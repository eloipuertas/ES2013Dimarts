    Game Guide / Progression / Tuning Mechs
    Tuning

    Every mech has seven systems you can tune: three common systems (used by all mechs) and four secondary systems (a unique set for each mech).

    Tuning these systems alters how your mech performs in battle. Tune your mechs to better suit your individual play style.

    Modifying Tuning Systems
    Tune Your Mech to Customize Its Performance

    Select a mech from your garage and click TUNING. The systems you can modify will appear in the modification window.

    The number of modification points you have available appears above the system slots. Add mod points to different systems to achieve the results you want.

    Every system has a base value that is constant across all mechs, plus a starting modification bonus that is unique to each mech.

    The tuning systems and starting values on your CR-T Recruit mech.
    Earn Modification Points by Gaining Pilot Levels

    Beginning at pilot level 5, you earn one modification point at each pilot level until you reach level 30, when you earn an additional 5 modification points, bringing your total to 30 points. You can apply all 30 points to every mech you own.

    At pilot level 30, you will have unlocked all 30 mod points.
    Unlock Extra Tuning Slots By Upgrading Your Mech

    A rank 1 mech can’t access every system slot. You unlock one column every time you upgrade to a new rank. Once a mech reaches rank 5, every column will be open, allowing you full access to system modifications.

    At Mech Rank 5, you will have unlocked all 10 system slots.
    Common Systems
    Weapons Loader, Heatsinks, and Armor

    All mechs share the three common systems, with one exception: the Technician gets the Healing Torch in place of the Weapons Loader.

    Common systems contain ten modification slots. At mech rank 1, six slots are open and the other four are unlocked as you upgrade your mech.

    Although all mechs share the common systems, the starting modification bonuses are unique to each mech.

    Common systems have different starting values for each mech. This example shows the starting values on the CR-T Recruit.
    Common System Modification Effects

    The following bonuses are granted with every modification point you add to the system.

    • Weapons Loader
      Each point increases your mech’s weapon firing rate by 1%.
    • Heatsinks
      Each point decreases your mech’s weapon heat generation by 1%.
    • Armor
      Each point increases your mech’s armor by 10 points.
    • Healing Torch (Technician only)
      Each point increases the reconstruction rate on the Helix Repair Torch by 0.9 armor per second and the deconstruction rate by 0.56 damage per second.
    The Technician is the only mech with the healing torch system in place of the weapons loader system.
    Secondary Systems
    Every Mech Has a Unique Set of Four Secondary Systems

    In addition to the three common systems, all mechs have eight secondary systems. A unique set of four of these systems can be modified on each mech. The others are locked.

    Tunable secondary systems contain six modification slots. As with common systems, the last four slots are unlocked as you upgrade your mech.

    All mechs have unique starting values assigned to secondary systems – even those you cannot modify.

    Secondary systems contain fewer slots than common systems. This example shows the secondary systems on the CR-T Recruit.
    Secondary System Modification Effects

    The following bonuses are granted with every modification point you add to the system.

    • Air Dynamics
      Increases flight speed by 1.2 meters per second, maximum height cap by 0.6 meters, and air acceleration by 0.15 meters per second per second.
    • Boost Thrusters
      Increases mech boost speed by 0.72 meters per second.
    • Cooling Units
      Decreases overheat recovery time by 0.65 seconds.
    • Fuel Tank
      Increases mech fuel capacity by 3.5 liters.
    • Generator
      Increases mech fuel regeneration by 0.5 liters per second.
    • Hydraulics
      Increases mech run speed by 0.5 meters per second, walk speed by 0.3 meters per second, and acceleration by 0.5 meters per second per second.
    • Radar
      Increases radar range by 15 meters.
    • Suspensions
      Decreases dodge cooldown time by 0.06 seconds.
